1. •Lectures 1 and 2 : Introduction to dynamical systems - fixed points, stability, phase portrait, trace-determinant diagram.

  2. •Lectures 3 and 4 : Dynamical systems analysis of a damped oscillator, bifurcation theory and normal forms. Introduction to saddle-node, transcritical and pitch-fork bifurcations.

  3. •Lectures 5 and 6 : Hopf bifurcation, fundamentals of linear systems - similarity transform (matrices with real eigenvalues and complex conjugate eigenvalues), matrix exponential, matrix normal forms, non-homogeneous linear systems and Duhamel’s formula, Lyapunov and asymptotic stability.

  4. •Lectures 7 and 8 : Adding perturbations to NSEs and linearization, fluid stability from Laplace transform perspective,  normal mode perturbations and its physical understanding, normal modes of a vibrating string.

  5. •Lectures 9 and 10 : Eigenanalysis of linear operators (with focus on differential operator), operator spectrum - discrete and continuous, Banach and Hilbert spaces, Sturm-Liouville BVP, adjoint of an operator and self-adjoint operators, Kelvin-Helmholtz and Rayleigh-Taylor instabilities, surface gravity waves, concept of dispersion relation. 

  6. •Lectures 11 and 12 : Inviscid stability theory - Rayleigh’s equation and its spectral properties, Squire’s theorem.

  7. •Lectures 13 and 14 : Understanding necessary condition and sufficient condition, Rayleigh’s inflextion point theorem, Fjorfoft’s theorem.

  8. •Lectures 15 and 16 : Rayleigh’s shear instability, Howard’s semi-circle theorem, Green’s function based analysis, Wave interaction theory, vorticity waves.

  9. •Lectures 17 and 18 : Critical layer theory, Tollmien’s inviscid solutions, Kelvin-cat eyes. 

  10. •Lectures 19 and 20 : Continuous spectrum in unbounded and bounded domains, singular/defective eigenfunctions, Balmforth-Morrison technique. 

  11. •Lectures 21 and 22 : Orr-Sommerfeld equation, Squire’s theorem.  

  12. •Lectures 23 and 24 : Rayleigh-Benard convection.
